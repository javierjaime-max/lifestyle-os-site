The Framework

Your life runs like an operating system.

Eight interdependent subsystems — Recovery, Metabolic, Physical, Cognitive, Temporal, Financial, Relational, and Strategic — determine your capacity, your performance, and your daily experience. When one fails, the others compensate. That compensation has a cost.

The diagnostic maps every subsystem, measures the gap between where you are and where your life demands you perform, and finds the single point of constraint that is limiting the whole system.

The Architecture

Eight systems. One operating system.

Every area of your life is governed by one of these subsystems. They interact. A failure in one cascades through the others. The diagnostic measures all eight — and finds which one is running as your constraint.

Recovery
Sleep, stress regulation, active recovery, rest. The maintenance window that determines what is available everywhere else.
Metabolic
Nutrition, hydration, supplementation, metabolic awareness. The fuel infrastructure for everything you produce.
Physical
Strength, cardiovascular capacity, mobility, training systems. The physical infrastructure that absorbs everything else.
Cognitive
Focus, decision quality, learning, state management. How your mind processes demand and operates under pressure.
Temporal
Schedule architecture, time protection, priority execution, delegation. Whether your time belongs to you or to whoever asks.
Financial
Income stability, cash flow, financial architecture, wealth building. The floor that determines whether your body feels safe.
Relational
Household dynamics, partnership, social infrastructure, conflict. The environment your biology runs in every day.
Strategic
Direction, goal architecture, execution, review, legacy. The command layer that determines what every other system is working toward.
